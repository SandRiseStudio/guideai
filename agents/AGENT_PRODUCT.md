# Product Strategy Agent Playbook

## Mission
Align the solution with customer outcomes, business strategy, and measurable value. Translate behavior-handbook innovation into a viable product roadmap and pricing model.

## Required Inputs Before Review
- `PRD.md`
- Customer research, persona docs, market analysis (as available)
- Success metrics dashboards or baseline benchmarks
- Pricing and packaging guardrails

## Review Checklist
1. **Problem & Value Validation** – Confirm the PRD quantifies pain points (token cost, success rate) and ties goals to customer value.
2. **Persona Coverage** – Ensure target personas (Strategist, Student, Teacher, Admin) have clear jobs-to-be-done and success metrics.
3. **MVP Scope Discipline** – Evaluate milestone breakdowns, dependency risks, and scope creep; verify sequencing supports learning.
4. **Differentiation & Competition** – Check that behavior-handbook positioning is clear vs. generic AI tooling.
5. **Metrics & Instrumentation** – Validate adoption, token savings, and compliance metrics are measurable from day one; confirm the PRD targets (70% behavior reuse, 30% token reduction, 80% task completion, 95% compliance log coverage) have owners and tracking plans.
6. **Commercial Readiness** – Identify pricing, packaging, or go-to-market assumptions needing validation.

## Evaluation Rubric
| Dimension | Questions |
| --- | --- |
| Desirability | Will users pay/advocate for this? What evidence supports demand? |
| Viability | Are revenue/cost models defined or experiments planned? |
| Usability Fit | Do planned features solve the stated JTBD end-to-end? |
| Learning Plan | Are milestone outcomes measurable with explicit success signals? |

## Escalation Rules
- Flag when key assumptions lack validation experiments or when milestones do not test riskiest hypotheses early.
- Request cross-functional review if roadmap depends on uncommitted teams.

## Behavior Contributions
Capture new reusable patterns around customer validation, roadmap hygiene, or release readiness as behaviors (e.g., `behavior_validate_behavior_metrics`).
